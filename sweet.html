    Sweet Delights

    Chocolate Recipe

    1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

    2. In a large bowl, sift together 1¾ cups of all-purpose flour, 2 cups of sugar, ¾ cup of cocoa powder, 1½ teaspoons of baking powder, 1½ teaspoons of baking soda, and 1 teaspoon of salt.

    3. Add 2 eggs, 1 cup of whole milk, ½ cup of vegetable oil, and 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract to the flour mixture. Mix until well combined.

    4. Stir in 1 cup of boiling water (the batter will be thin). Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans.

    5. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

    6. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then remove from pans to wire racks to cool completely.

    7. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the chocolate frosting. In a medium bowl, combine ½ cup of softened butter, ⅔ cup of cocoa powder, 3 cups of powdered sugar, ⅓ cup of milk, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and spreadable.

    8. Once the cakes are cool, spread frosting on the top of one cake layer, place the second layer on top, and continue frosting the top and sides of the cake.

    9. Garnish with chocolate shavings or sprinkles if desired. Let the cake sit for a while before serving to let the flavors meld together.

    Apple Pie

    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Get one package of store-bought pie crusts or prepare your homemade crust.

    2. Peel, core, and slice 6 medium-sized apples. Mix the apple slices with ¾ cup of sugar, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and ¼ teaspoon of ground nutmeg in a large bowl.

    3. In a small bowl, mix together 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Sprinkle this mixture over the bottom of the pie crust to prevent sogginess.

    4. Pour the apple mixture into the pie crust, spreading it evenly. Dot the top with small pieces of butter (about 1 tablespoon).

    5. Cover the filling with the second pie crust. Seal the edges by crimping with a fork or your fingers. Cut small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

    6. For a golden-brown finish, lightly brush the top crust with a beaten egg and sprinkle a little sugar over it.

    7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

    8. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This allows the filling to set.

    9. Serve the apple pie with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, if desired.
